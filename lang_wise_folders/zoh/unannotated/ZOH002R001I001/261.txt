Noun-noun compounds are vastly more common in the corpus than
Adjective-Noun phrases. Many names of plants and animals are nominal
compounds, and it seems to be a common way to add new terms to the
language. The syntax of nominal compounds is analogous to that in English:
the first noun modifies the second.
