9.5.3. Only
The clitic +štaa? can attach to any word class in MIG Zoque, except
probably the intensifiers. Its general meaning is the same in all cases: it limits
the set or extent or scope of the action. It attaches outside (to the right of) a
plural clitic (9.22 i) or postposition (v amd viii), but inside the adverbial clitic
+7am (NOW), shown in example (ii). Example (iii) shows that even when
attached to the verb complex, it can serve to delimit one of the verb's
arguments. In (iv), +štaa? is limiting the scope of the action: they just sit, they
don't work. Example (v) shows the clitic limiting a noun phrase to one out of a
possible two, and example (vi) shows it setting a precise limit, when attached to
a number. Examples (vii and viii) show +štaa? with a locative deictic and a
postpositional phrase.
