The idiom cak.?əm-, 'to remain', or 'to be left', accounts for the vast
majority of passives found in the corpus (examples i and ii, below). It may be a
calque on the Spanish expression quedarse, 'to stay; to remain'. Another idiom
that appears to be a calque on a Spanish se expression is pək=nək.?əm-, 'to
get along', llevarse in Spanish (example iii). pək=nək, 'get=go', is the most
common way to say 'to take' in MIG Zoque. Literally, the stem would mean 'to
be taken'. Also note that the construction shown in (iii) is transitive.
