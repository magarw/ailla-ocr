Adjectival or existential relative clauses appear after the nouns that they
modify. There are two alternate forms of the relative clause marker: +V?k and
+pǝ?k. The vowel-initial alternate follows words or phrases that end in vowels
(as all verb complexes do, since all the inflectional suffixes are vowel-final).
The consonant-initial alternate follows words that end in consonants, and thus
appears with many of the adjectives. Relative clauses are discussed further in
section 11.1.
