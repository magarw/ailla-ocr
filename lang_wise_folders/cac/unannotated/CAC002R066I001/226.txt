nitzuchih, vtr-dir. To move something flush against something. Ex: nitzuch ha p'ah, Get
yourself closer! (to a fire, the wall, etc.)
nitz'ek'ih, vtr-dir. To move something to the side.
nitzkotih, vtr-dir. = nitzkutih. To move something towards a goal (but not all the way).
nitza', exclam. Move it! (any of the above).
niwak, adj. Big, large, e.g., large fruits. Cf. niwan, niwakil, nit.
niwakil, adj. Large, but not exceptionally so.
niwaktak, adj (plural). Many large things.
niwak hi'ih, n ('ixim). Spanish "jilote." = niwaki'ih. Corn ear sprout not quite developed into a
roasting ear; kernels not yet fully formed. Ex: sniwaki'ihal = sniwak hi'ihal, the largest of the
jilotes. Cf. hi'ih.
niwak hop'es te', n (te'). Plant name. Br 11494. Tournefortia acutifolia. Cf. hop'es te'.
niwak kap' te', n (te'). Plant name. Br 11516, 11641, 11665. Saurauia conzattii, S. oreophila, S.
selerorum, S. scabrida. Cf. kap' te'.
niwak sakilap', n (te'). Plant name. Br 8489, 8683. Buddleia skutchii. Cf. kotak sakilap', tanh
sakilap'.
niwak tut, n ('anh). Plant name. Literally, Big Bean. A large bean that climbs wrapping around
things, planted in milpa or by a tree trunk. Spanish "frijol champorote." Br 11566. Phaseolus
formosus. Scarlet Runner Bean.
niwak winak, n (winh). Literally, big person. Spanish "gente fina." People who live in
Guatemala City, or North Americans, or Spanish. Not in reference to any local people, including
rich plantation owners or important men. Cf. niwan winak, big man in size.
niwak yak' chej 'anh, n (anh). Plant name. Br 11529. Senecio sp. Cf. yak' chej 'anh.
niwan, adj. Many, or very big in size, e.g., a huge rock outcrop. Spanish "bastante." Cf. niwak.
Ex: niwan winak, big man (in size; cf. niwak winak).
niwan 'ak'lik, n (lum). Place name. Literally, Big Plain. Llano Grande, San Mateo, an
aldea where Mateano Chuj is spoken.
