xek'el, n (lum). Place name. Xegüel, a caserío of Sebep (sewep'), San Mateo. Also, Arroyo

xel-, vpo root. Split, bi- (or multi-)partite as if split.
xelan xelan, stv phrase. Split, or bipartite as if split, e.g., a cow's ear split for
identification. = xalan xalan.
xelanh (+N), num cl (2d, O26). Finger-like sections of something, as if it had been split
into sections. Ex: cha xelanh, split into two (as an animal's ear split for identification). ho
xelanh xil te', a leaf with five sections like fingers.
xelajup', n (lum). Place name: Quetzaltenango (Xela). Now being replaced by ketzaltenánhgoh.
xelajup'al chanh, n phrase (k'apak). Skirts from Quetzaltenango.
xelajup'al xumpil, n phrase (ch'anh). A class of hats made only in Quetzaltenango.

xemel, n ('ixim). A tamal with whole beans, made by mixing undried uncooked beans with corn
dough in a large ball, then pulling off pieces and wrapping and steaming them. Cf. k'alap', a
ground bean tamal. Ex: xemel winh, his tamal. xemelal k'inh, tamales for a festival.
xen, n (nok'; 'ajtzo', 'ix). = xe'en. Long-legged mosquitos. Cf. 'us, other mosquitos.
